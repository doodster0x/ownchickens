Source birds yourself

Good first sources include reputable local farms, hatcheries, feed stores, and experienced keepers with clean setups. Ask about age, breed, health, vaccination status, and whether the birds are already laying.

Use our flock sourcing service

If you want help, we can look for beginner friendly birds near you and point you toward practical options. The goal is simple: healthy hens that fit your yard, rules, and setup.

Bring birds home after setup

Have the coop, run, feed, water, bedding, and local approval ready first. Birds are easy when the system is ready before they arrive.

Own Chickens is an educational starting point. Always verify current rules with your city or county before buying birds or building a coop.
